Executive Summary

This roadmap globalizes the subscription optimization methodology proven in the 2025 USA engagement across all 40+ markets. The USA program delivered a +13.82 percentage point approval rate improvement and $9.8M in documented value through card updater services, clean address data, and MIT/CIT+OTI implementation.

The 2026 strategy deploys 10 interconnected initiatives across $414M+ in annual payment volume processed through WorldPay (44 markets), Nuvei (APAC), and Checkout.com (Middle East). Every initiative applies globally. Market-specific sequencing exists only for rollout scheduling, not scope limitation.

Parallel infrastructure work addresses a critical gap: declined payment records are not retained at the order level. The Unity database platform MUST persist ALL payment attempts (approved AND declined) at the order level with full context including processor attribution, decline reason codes, and subscription linkage.

Data Infrastructure Gap

Critical foundation issue: declined payments not retained at order level, creating a $500K-$2M analytics blind spot

Current State: The Problem

When a payment is declined, no ODP record is created. The decline data exists only in UnityvTellerLog without order context. This means:

Analytics Blind Spot
Cannot calculate true approval rates at order level. $500K-$2M in missed optimization opportunities.
CSR Visibility Gap
Support cannot see why a customer's order failed. Increased handle time and customer frustration.
Incomplete Fraud Models
FraudSight and retry logic cannot reference historical decline patterns without order-linked data.
Reconciliation Gaps
