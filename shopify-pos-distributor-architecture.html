Control Hierarchy: Who Controls What

Shopify uses two independent permission layers: Store-level permissions (Shopify Admin access) and POS roles (POS app access). Regional managers get both. Distributors get POS roles only.

HQ Admin (Payment Ops / IT) FULL SHOPIFY ADMIN
Store owner or staff with all store-level permissions. 2-3 people.
Products and Pricing
Create, edit, and delete all products. Set prices (store-wide). Manage variants, images, descriptions. Pricing changes apply to ALL locations.
Locations
Create and configure POS Pro locations. Assign products to locations. Set which products are available where. Manage POS subscriptions.
Payment Settings
Shopify Payments configuration. Payout bank accounts. Tax settings. Payment capture mode. Fraud protection rules.
Staff and Roles
Create POS roles (Distributor, Manager). Create admin roles with limited permissions for regional managers. Assign staff to locations.
Analytics and Finance
View all reports, payouts, settlements. Revenue by location, staff, channel. Export data. Manage billing.
Online Store
Theme, checkout, shipping, subscriptions, apps, domains, SEO. Full control over every sales channel.

Regional Manager LIMITED ADMIN + POS MANAGER ROLE
Admin staff with restricted store-level permissions + POS Manager role. One per region.
CAN: Manage Product Availability
Control which products appear at their location via inventory/availability settings. Toggle items on/off for their POS location. Needs "Products" store-level permission.
CAN: Manage POS Staff
Add/remove distributors at their location. Assign PINs. Set POS roles. Needs "Manage Point of Sale staff" POS permission.
CAN: Process Returns

CAN: View Location Orders
See all orders from their assigned location. Cannot see other locations unless given "Manage orders at all locations" permission.
CANNOT: Change Pricing
Prices are store-wide in Shopify. Even with Products permission, editing a price changes it at ALL locations. HQ controls pricing.
CANNOT: Payment/Finance Settings
No access to Shopify Payments config, payout accounts, tax settings, analytics, or financial reports. HQ only.
Shopify constraint: Product pricing is store-wide, not per-location. If a regional manager edits a product price, the change affects every location. To enforce regional pricing differences, use Shopify Plus B2B Catalogs or Markets (both included with Plus). For most MLM operations, HQ sets pricing centrally and regional managers control product availability at their location.

Distributor POS-ONLY STAFF (PIN ACCESS)
POS app only. No Shopify admin access. Unlimited accounts at zero cost per staff.
CAN: Process Sales
Log in via PIN. Browse the product catalog at their assigned location. Ring up customers. Accept card payment via Tap to Pay or Shopify reader.
CAN: Capture Customer Info
Add customer email/phone at checkout for marketing lists and order receipts. Cannot view full customer profiles.
CAN: Auto-Attribute Sales
Every sale auto-attributes to their staff_id via POS Pro. Shows in webhook payload as attributed_staffs. Commission tracked automatically.
CANNOT: Add/Remove Products
Product catalog is set by HQ and managed at the location level. Distributor sells from whatever catalog is available at their assigned location.
CANNOT: Change Prices or Discounts
No discount capability unless the POS role explicitly allows it. No access to modify any pricing. Sells at the price set by HQ.
CANNOT: Access Admin/Orders/Reports
No Shopify admin. Cannot see order history, analytics, other staff, inventory counts, financial data, or any backend information.

What the Distributor Actually Sees

The distributor experience is intentionally minimal. They open the Shopify POS app, enter their PIN, and sell. They never see the Shopify admin, other orders, analytics, or any backend data.

📱
Step 1: Open App
Opens Shopify POS on their phone. Sees PIN entry screen.
🔢
Step 2: Enter PIN
Enters their unique 4-6 digit PIN. Auto-assigns their staff_id for attribution.
🛒
Step 3: Browse Catalog
Sees only the products available at their location. Prices set by HQ. Cannot modify.
💳
Step 4: Accept Payment
Customer taps or inserts card. Shopify Payments processes at 2.15% + 30 cents. Done.
✅
Step 5: Sale Attributed
Sale auto-attributed to distributor. Commission calculated. Visible in Unicity portal.
Key point for leadership: Distributors never touch the Shopify admin. They cannot see revenue, other distributors' sales, inventory levels, financial settings, or any backend data. The POS app is their only interface. Everything else is controlled by HQ or the regional manager.

Commission tracking requirement: To map POS sales back to Unicity distributor IDs, you need a naming convention or custom field mapping.
